The Lore

Every character I make lives somewhere. Every world I build has rules, myths, and things that go strange in the fog. This is where the stories live.

Who Is Volara, Really

She was a duchess once, in the proper sense — titles, estates, obligations. She found the obligations the least interesting part. The title she kept. The estate she converted into something considerably more chaotic.

She is one of the founding members of the Soot Circle, the secret society that built the Brass Foundry from nothing but a derelict furnace, a bad idea, and the collective refusal of three people to be told what they were allowed to make.

She runs her guild — the Airship — from a berth at the Skyrail Docks that appears on no official manifest. Her quarters in the Cogspire rotate on a schedule only she controls. She has not faded into legend the way the other founders have. She has intensified.

The Duchess Who Didn't Die

Ferrath's upper classes insist the original duchess — the first of the Soot Circle — perished decades ago in a scandalous accident involving a prototype airship and an ill-advised bet. There is a grave with her name on it in the Heights' most prestigious cemetery.

Foundry members do not go to that cemetery. They have no reason to mourn someone they keep seeing at the bottom of staircases, disappearing around corners, leaving notes in handwriting that matches too perfectly the founding documents.

Whether she is a ghost, a myth, a very dedicated series of descendants, or simply a woman who has always been excellent at not dying — the Foundry does not say. Her legend, like her, refuses to be laid to rest.

The Philosophy of the Airship

Those who earn a place on the Airship find themselves in the company of someone who demands excellence, rewards chaos, and has absolutely no patience for anyone who leaves their morality at her doorstep and then asks for it back.

The Airship moves where it pleases and answers to nothing below the cloud line. Its berth at the Skyrail Docks does not appear on any official manifest. You find it when it wants to be found.

"Soar, or stay behind."

— Volara, on the philosophy of the Airship
